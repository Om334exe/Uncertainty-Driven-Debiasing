# Title: Mitigating Cognitive Biases in Large Language Model Diagnostics: A Swarm-Assisted Expert System Approach

## Abstract
Large Language Models (LLMs) have shown immense promise in clinical decision support and **Expert Systems** (one of the key emerging areas of Intelligent System Design). However, they frequently suffer from cognitive biases—such as **Anchoring**, **Availability Heuristics**, and **Base-Rate Neglect**—leading to premature diagnostic closure. We present *CogDiag*, a novel Streamlit-based healthcare diagnostic application that utilizes a simulated "Cognitive Swarm" of targeted agents to detect and mathematically penalize diagnostic biases in real-time. By applying targeted discounting on the raw confidence of LLM inferences, we demonstrate a resilient clinical diagnostic pipeline.

## 1. Introduction
* **Context**: Real-world applications in Healthcare and Intelligent System Design.
* **The Problem**: Pre-trained transformer models lack structured epidemiological grounding. If a user inputs "chest pain and anxiety", the model immediately anchors to psychological models, neglecting life-threatening organic causes like Myocarditis (Anchoring Bias). 
* **The Proposed Solution**: A novel Streamlit application (CogDiag) that acts as an interpretability and bias-mitigation layer. 

## 2. Methodology & System Architecture
* **The Streamlit Application**: Explain how the UI allows clinicians to input patient metrics and instantly see both the *Raw Baseline* prediction and the *Mitigated* prediction.
* **The "Cognitive Swarm" Metric**: 
    1. **Anchoring Detection Agent**: Penalizes rapid high-confidence conclusions drawn from a single keyword.
    2. **Availability Heuristic Monitor**: Adjusts weights based on the occurrence rates in the model's primary training data versus regional clinical baselines.
* **Formulas**: In the paper, you can present the equation used in the app's backend: 
  `Mitigated_Confidence = Confidence * (1 - (Bias_Risk * \alpha))` 
  where `\alpha` is the user-defined intervention threshold.
